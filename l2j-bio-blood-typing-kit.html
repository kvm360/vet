Single-Use Kit

Simple Testing Procedure

Transfer Blood Sample

Using the supplied plastic pipette, transfer a small amount of whole blood (approximately 10 microliters) to the testing card.

Add Buffer Solution

Put one drop of the supplied buffer solution (approximately 45μL) into each well of the testing card.

Mix with Wooden Stick

Mix the solution in each well with the provided wooden stick for about 15 seconds. Use a different stick for each well to prevent cross-contamination.

Read Results

The results can be read from the test kit within 2 minutes. Do not read the test results after 5 minutes as they may become inaccurate.

Why Blood Typing is Important

Avoid Transfusion Reactions

Dogs have naturally occurring blood antigens. Mismatched blood can cause serious reactions like hemolysis, shock, or even fatal complications.

Scientifically Validated

Kai 1 and Kai 2 blood types were discovered through advanced methods and published in the peer-reviewed journal PLOS ONE (2017).

Emergency Preparedness

Knowing your dog's blood type in advance avoids delays during emergencies and ensures safe, compatible treatment when seconds matter.

Enhanced Safety with Kai Blood Typing

Kai 1 and Kai 2 are newly discovered blood types that can explain hidden transfusion risks. By identifying these types before transfusion, we can significantly reduce the chances of adverse reactions. When combined with the existing DEA1 test, Kai blood typing provides an extra layer of safety - giving your beloved pet the best chance when it matters most.
